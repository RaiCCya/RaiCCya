Politeness Levels (敬語 Keigo)

Japanese uses different speech levels to show respect and social hierarchy. Here's a quick overview:

Plain (Informal) Form

Used with close friends, family, and in casual speech.

  • 食べる (taberu) — to eat
  • 行く (iku) — to go

Polite Form (ます/です)

The default polite form used in everyday conversation and formal settings.

  • 食べます (tabemasu)
  • 行きます (ikimasu)

Honorific Form (尊敬語, そんけいご)

Used to show respect to the person you are talking about.

  • 召し上がる (meshiagaru) — to eat (honorific)
  • いらっしゃる (irassharu) — to go, come, be (honorific)

Humble Form (謙譲語, けんじょうご)

Used to humble oneself or one’s in-group in relation to others.

  • いただく (itadaku) — to receive/eat (humble)
  • 参る (mairu) — to go/come (humble)

Casual Contractions & Slang

Common in informal speech, reflecting personality and tone.

  • 食べちゃう (tabechau) — casual contraction of 食べてしまう (to finish eating)
  • 行っちゃう (icchau) — casual contraction of 行ってしまう (to leave/go)
