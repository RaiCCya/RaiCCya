🇯🇵🇵🇭 Japanese & Filipino History

🌸 Japanese History Snapshot

Japan’s story spans from the Jōmon period to the modern Reiwa era. Samurai, Shintoism, the Meiji Restoration, and technological revolutions define a journey of resilience and reinvention.

Did you know? The Heian period (794–1185) is known for the rise of court poetry and the world’s first novel: *The Tale of Genji* by Murasaki Shikibu.

⛩ Cultural Themes

Tradition and aesthetics like *wabi-sabi*, seasonal festivals, and language levels (keigo) shape modern Japanese identity. Many customs, from bowing to cherry blossom viewing, reflect respect, impermanence, and harmony.

🏛 Filipino History Snapshot

From the pre-colonial barangay system to Spanish colonization and the 1986 People Power Revolution, the Philippines holds a rich tapestry of resistance, community, and identity.

Highlight: The *Baybayin* script, once widely used before Spanish arrival, is now being revived by a new generation.

🎎 Interwoven Histories

Japan and the Philippines have crossed paths in war, diplomacy, and migration. Today, shared culture blooms in media, education, and food — from anime to adobo bentōs.
